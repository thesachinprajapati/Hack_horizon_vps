Fundamental Rights of Indian Citizens

The Constitution offers all citizens, individually and collectively, some basic freedoms. These are guaranteed in the Constitution of India under Articles 12 to 35 in Part III.

Right to Equality (Articles 14–18)

The Right to Equality ensures that all individuals are treated equally before the law, fostering fairness and justice in society. It prohibits discrimination.

Right to Freedom (Articles 19–22)

The Right to Freedom grants individuals a variety of essential liberties, promoting personal and social growth. It includes the freedom of speech and expression.

Right against Exploitation (Articles 23–24)

This right protects people from being forced into exploitative or unfair labor conditions and fights against social evils like human trafficking.

Right to Freedom of Religion (Articles 25–28)

The Right to Freedom of Religion guarantees that individuals can freely follow, practice, and propagate the religion of their choice.

Cultural and Educational Rights (Articles 29–30)

These rights safeguard the cultural identities of minority groups, ensuring that their traditions, languages, and heritage are preserved.

Right to Constitutional Remedies (Articles 32–35)

The Right to Constitutional Remedies empowers individuals to seek justice if their Fundamental Rights are violated.

Right to Privacy (Implied under Article 21)

The Right to Privacy ensures that individuals have control over their personal information and protects their dignity.

Know your rights and use them to protect your dignity, equality, and justice.
